Automated Hydroponic Garden with Raspberry Pi:

Previous Next



In my second year of geography I learned about issues facing the conventional farming system and its unsustainable practices. For a while I was looking for potential solutions to this problem and stumbled upon Hydroponics and soil-less growing. I found it fascinating that food could be grown healthy and faster without the need of soil, as well as being more sustainable all at the same time.

This got me thinking of whether or not it was possible to build my own system. For the summer of 2016 I learned a lot about electricity, circuits, arduino, raspberry pi, hydroponic nutrients and plant biology, thinking about all the necessary components needed and what would be feasible to automate with my new found programming skils. Through making this project I learned a lot and was able to grow kale without the need of human input for a whole month while I was off to Japan.
Link to the Github


Bluetooth RGB Lighting System:

Previous Next



During my third year at university I continued to tinker with my hydroponic setup, however, I wanted to explore the ability of using web-technology as an interface. One of the most crucial aspects of a hydroponic setup is Lighting and I dived deeper into communication from a micro controller to a webserver. My idea guided me towards using a bluetooth module with arduino to send information from a node.js webserver.
