Automated Hydroponic Garden with Raspberry Pi:

Previous Next



In my second year of geography I learned about issues facing the conventional farming system and its unsustainable practices. For a while I was looking for potential solutions to this problem and stumbled upon Hydroponics and soil-less growing. I found it fascinating that food could be grown healthy and faster without the need of soil, as well as being more sustainable all at the same time.

After taking a couple of programming courses and seeing its potential, this got me thinking of whether or not it was possible to build my own system. For the summer of 2016 I learned a lot about electricity, circuits, arduino, raspberry pi, hydroponic systems and plant biology, thinking about all the necessary components needed and what would be feasible to automate with my new found programming skils. Through making this project I learned a lot and was able to grow kale without the need of human input for a whole month while I was off to Japan.

Kingston School Meals Policy Document - 2018

In my fourth year of university I participated in a seminar class on sustainable food systems. For this class our job was to create a policy document on the current issues of school meals programs at multiple scales of government, working with a team of 16 students. We looked at what is being done internationally, the national scale in Canada, as well as the provincial and local scale. This document was to inform local organizations focused on school meals programs in Kingston about how the issues are being tackled at larger scale degrees and highlight areas needing further research to address the sustainability of current programs.

Link to the Report

Evaluating Barriers to entry of Solar Panels in the City of Groningen - 2017

In my third year of university, I was fortunate enough to participate in an exchange program at the university of Groningen. One of the classes focused on designing a research project focused on spatial relations to the city of Groningen. I worked with a team of 3 students wanting to understand the socially precieved barriers of solar panels in the city and what were fundamental challenges to its further implementation.

We collected data through surveying the city population depending on different demographic factors, such as rented homes vs. owner occupied, and whether or not residents had solar panels. We used the survey to analyze the differences in perception between the groups and what they believed to be the issue with solar panels.

Finally, we presented background information on the current state of renewable and solar energy in the Netherlands, as well as our findings and what were key limitations and potential solutions to these problems.
