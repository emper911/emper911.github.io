Who am I?



I am a Japanese-American, born and raised in the Greater New York City area. My mother is Japanese and my father is Japanese-American. When I was 10 years old I moved to Japan going to Tokyo International School and later Yokohama International School. At these schools I had the opportunity to meet people from all over the world and when I came back to New York at 16 years old I was longing for a different experience, deciding to go north for university.

I am currently an international student attending Queen's University in Canada. I major in Geography and Computer Science as ways for me to make sense of my experiences and to grasp at an understanding of the complex world we live in. Through studying geography I have developed a macro-perspective on a variety of subjects such as history, politics, economics, climate change, sustainability and public health, centered around the built environment and globalization.

In combinitation with Computer Science, I am deeply interested in technology and its impact on city spaces. Smart cities in combination with IoT technologies have a diversity of implications and may allow us to re-imagine urban space like never before. These developments have the potential to greatly improve the efficiency of infrastructure and redesign urban environments to be focused on liveability; however, their is cause for concern over the logistics of ensuring privacy and dissuading maliscious use of information.

In my own pursuits I hope to contribute to the positive outcome of these technologies and focus on bettering the lives of individuals through rethinking and integrating natural & human landscapes with the digital space.
