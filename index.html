About me



I am Japanese-American, born and raised in the Greater New York City area. My mother is Japanese and my father is Japanese-American. When I was 10 years old I moved to Japan going to Tokyo International School and later Yokohama International School. At these schools I had the opportunity to meet people from all over the world and when coming back to New York at age 16, I was longing for a new experience.

I am currently an international student attending Queen's University in Kington, Canada. I major in Geography, minor in Computer Science and I am also pursuing a certificate in GIS. I study these subjects as ways of understanding my own experiences of globalized processes, contingent upon the rapid development of technology. Through studying geography I have developed a macro and spatial perspective on a variety of subjects such as history, politics, economics, climate change, sustainability and public health, in the context of the built environment and globalization.

In combination with Computer Science and GIS, I am interested in data-driven technologies and their future implications on reimagining city spaces. The integration of GIS as a means for analysis and data visualization, with IoT (Internet of Things) technologies have a diversity of implications in changing the current order of urban environments. These data-driven technologies have the potential to greatly improve the efficiency of infrastructure, allow for the redesign of urban spaces for liveability and help manifest the Smart City.

In my own pursuits I hope to contribute to the positive outcomes of these technologies for bettering the lives of individuals, through reimagining human-made landscapes, by the means of leveraging big-data.
